Anycdote

Anycdote is an iOS application that allows users to anonymously share stories in the form of audio files by pinning them all around Manhattan. Inspired by Humans of New York, users are able to listen to stories pinned around them and admins can manage pinned stories within the actual application. Anycdote is currently in beta, if you'd like to join the beta or are interested in learning more, click here

  • Client: Anycdote
  • Date: April 2015 - present
  • Service: iOS Development

Hashtack

Hashtack, a startup that pitched at the Seattle TechCrunch last year, is a photo- and video-sharing app that brings together Facebook, Instagram and Twitter into one medium. Users can like, comment and zoom into pictures, as well as repost them. This app is great for social media managers who want to reach out to their audience through various social media platforms. I mainly handled front end development for this application.

  • Client: Hashtack
  • Date: September 2014 - February 2015
  • Service: iOS & Web Development

Quick Cash PSU

Quick Cash is a campus-based service that allows students to trade their electronics for cash. Quick Cash representatives will even come to the students' doors and pick up the devices. All the student has to do is fill out a form on the website and Quick Cash takes care of the rest.

  • Client: Quick Cash PSU
  • Date: March 2015
  • Service: Web Development

Content Watch

Content Watch allows users to save sections of websites using a screenshot tool. Each time a change is made to a saved secion of a website, the user is notified via email. Content Watch was created MHacks 3 and it won our team the Evernote API prize as well as a spot in the top ten!

  • Client: Hackathon Project
  • Date: January 2014
  • Service: Web Development
